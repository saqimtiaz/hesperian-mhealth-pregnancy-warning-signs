Too much bleeding
before the placenta comes out

  • Ask the mother to squat and push the placenta out.
  • Ask her to urinate. A full bladder prevents the womb from contracting.
  • Put the baby to the mother's breast to start suckling. If the baby will not suckle, have the mother roll her nipples. This will help her womb contract and push the placenta out.
  • Give 10 units of oxytocin by injection in the buttock or thigh, or give 400 mcg of misoprostol by mouth, one time only.
A woman who is bleeding a lot is in danger of shock. Check her for signs of shock.
